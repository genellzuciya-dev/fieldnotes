01 — Painted Desert, AZ

Globemallow & badlands

Sphaeralcea sp.

The banded hills behind the bloom are Late Triassic mudstone — laid down when this desert was a humid floodplain near the equator. The red bands are oxidized iron; the grey ones aren't. That color difference is a climate signal, roughly 220 million years old.

Chinle Formation · ~220 Ma
02 — Painted Desert, AZ

First on broken ground

Sphaeralcea sp.

Globemallow is a pioneer species — it takes the disturbed, mineral-poor ground nothing else wants. Here it is doing that on the eroded remains of a wetter world, indifferent to the fact.

Arid adapted · disturbed ground
03 — Hawaiʻi [TK: island]

Red-crested cardinal

Paroaria coronata

Native to South America, not to this canopy. Introduced in the 1930s and entirely at home now. Photographing it is photographing a novel ecosystem assembling itself in real time, out of pieces we moved around.

Introduced c. 1930s · established
04 — Hawaiʻi [TK: island]

Common myna

Acridotheres tristis

Brought here in 1865 to eat armyworms in the cane fields — a biological control that worked, then kept going. It is now one of the most successful birds on earth, and it is sitting on our wire, watching.

Introduced 1865 · established
05 — [TK: coast]

Gooseneck barnacles

Pollicipes sp.

They live where every wave is a hydraulic event that would tear most animals off the rock. They hold on with a protein cement that materials scientists have been trying to reverse-engineer for decades — and they feed by reaching into the water that is trying to kill them.

Surf zone · wave-swept · filter feeder
06 — Hawaiʻi [TK: island]

Cattle egret

Bubulcus ibis

Released here in 1959 to eat the flies off livestock — the same bargain the islands made with the myna a century earlier. The bird kept its end of it, and then kept going. It follows cattle for a living: hooves stir the grass, insects jump, the egret is already there.

Introduced 1959 · pest control · established
07 — Hawaiʻi [TK: island]

The reason the egrets came

Bos taurus

Cattle reached these islands in 1793, a gift to Kamehameha I. A royal kapu protected them from being killed, so they multiplied into feral herds — and a generation later Mexican vaqueros were brought in to manage them. The paniolo were working cattle in Hawaiʻi before the American cowboy existed.

Introduced 1793 · pasture · paniolo country
08 — Scotland [TK: locality]

Highland cow

Bos taurus taurus

The fringe is not decorative. It is a double coat — an oily outer layer that sheds rain, a downy one beneath that traps heat — which lets the breed carry less body fat than cattle bred for softer weather, and eat rough forage other cows won't touch. Everything about the animal is an answer to Scottish weather.

Native breed · double coat · rough grazing
09 — Scotland [TK: locality]

Calves, unbothered

Bos taurus taurus

Born woolly and stay that way. Highland calves are famously hardy — dropped outdoors, raised outdoors, and left to grow the coat that will do the rest of the work for the next twenty years.

Outwintered · hardy · long-lived breed
